Academic Papers

My Most Quintessential Writings Summarized Here

Google's Tracking Accuracy

A collaborative research effort to determine accuracy of Google's profile tracking and user perceptions. I created some of sections of the survey, and did half of the statistical analysis. Long story short, Google is overall highly accurate. People do care about privacy, but not enought to change habits.

Privacy Policies are Failures

An independent research project using text analysis techniques to determine readbility of privacy policies. Privacy policies are graded using the Flesch Ease score to rate policies as easy vs hard. The results showed actionable changes could be made to the language of privacy policies to make them easier to read such as using direct language.

A Critique of Paternalism

A messy argument done for a political philosophy paper. Despite its many flaws, this paper perfectly represents my love for privacy through disdain for authority. It argues that a paternal state requires some surveillance to determine "what's best". Surveillance itself alters behaviors, and people deserve privacy to preserve behaviors under freedom.
